Famous Places in Uttar Pradesh

Taj Mahal

One of the Seven Wonders of the World, the Taj Mahal in Agra is a symbol of love built by Emperor Shah Jahan for his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum located on the right bank of the river Yamuna in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India. It was commissioned in 1631 by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan to house the tomb of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died in childbirth in 1631. The construction of the mausoleum was completed by about 1638–39, and the entire complex was finished by 1648. The Taj Mahal is a masterpiece of Mughal architecture and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by a crenellated wall. It also includes a mosque and a guest house, and the mausoleum itself is the centrepiece of a 17-hectare (42-acre) complex. The Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983 and is widely considered one of the most beautiful buildings ever created.



Varanasi Ghats

Varanasi, one of the holiest cities, is known for its sacred ghats where people perform rituals and witness the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti. Ahilya Ghat by the Ganges, Varanasi Chet Singh Ghat in Varanasi Kedar Ghat in Varanasi Ghats in Varanasi are riverfront steps leading to the banks of the Ganges river. The city has 84 ghats. Most of the ghats are bathing and puja ceremonial ghats, while two ghats, Manikarnika and Harishchandra, are used exclusively as cremation sites. Most of the ghats in Varanasi were rebuilt in the 18th century under the maratha patronage. The patrons of current ghats are Maharajas of Benares, Marathas, Shindes (Scindias), Holkars, Bhonsles and Peshwes (Peshwas). Many ghats are associated with legends or mythologies while other ghats have private histories and users. A morning boat ride on the Ganges along the ghats is a popular visitor attraction.



Ayodhya

The birthplace of Lord Rama, Ayodhya holds immense significance in Hinduism and is home to the newly built Ram Mandir. A terror plot targeting the Ram Temple in Ayodhya was foiled on March 3, 2025, with the arrest of Abdul Rahman in Faridabad. The suspect, a resident of Uttar Pradesh, was linked to Pakistan's ISI and had conducted reconnaissance of the temple multiple times. Additionally, the Ayodhya Municipal Corporation is dealing with an unprecedented amount of footwear left by devotees visiting the Ram Temple following the Maha Kumbh, with nearly 30 trollies of shoes and sandals being cleared daily.



Sarnath

Famous as the place where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon, Sarnath is a major Buddhist pilgrimage site near Varanasi.Sarnath is a town located 8 kilometers northeast of Varanasi, near the confluence of the Ganges and the Varuna rivers in Uttar Pradesh, India. It is historically significant as the place where Gautama Buddha first taught Buddhism after attaining enlightenment at Bodh Gaya. Sarnath is also known as Deer Park and is one of the eight most important pilgrimage sites for Buddhists. The Lion Capital of Ashoka, which is the basis of India's state emblem, was part of an ancient column built in Sarnath



Agra Fort

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Agra Fort is an architectural marvel of the Mughal era, housing several palaces and gardens.Agra Fort is a large 16th-century fortress located on the Yamuna River in the historic city of Agra, in Uttar Pradesh state, north-central India. It was established by the Mughal emperor Akbar in 1565 and served as the seat of government for the Mughal Dynasty until 1638 when the capital was shifted to Delhi. The fort is a powerful fortress built of red sandstone and encompasses a 2.5-kilometer-long enclosure wall, with numerous exquisite buildings inside, including palaces, mosques, and audience halls



Bara Imambara

One of Lucknow's most famous historical structures, the Bara Imambara is known for its unique architecture and the intriguing Bhool Bhulaiya. Bara Imambara, also known as Asafi Imambara, is an imambara complex in Lucknow, India. It was built by Asaf-ud-Daula, the Nawab of Awadh, in 1784. The complex includes the large Asfi mosque, the Bhul-bhulaiya (the labyrinth), and Bowli, a steep well with running water.



Mathura

Considered the birthplace of Lord Krishna, Mathura is an important pilgrimage site, especially during Holi and Janmashtami.In Hinduism, the birthplace of Krishna, one of the main deities in that religion, is believed to be located in Mathura at the Krishna Janmasthan Temple Complex.[5] It is one of the Sapta Puri, the seven cities considered holy by Hindus, also is called Mokshyadayni Tirth. The Kesava Deo Temple was built in ancient times on the site of Krishna's birthplace (an underground prison). Mathura was the capital of the kingdom of Surasena, ruled by Kamsa, the maternal uncle of Krishna. Mathura is part of the Krishna circuit (Mathura, Vrindavan, Barsana, Govardhan, Kurukshetra, Dwarka and Bhalka). Krishna Janmashtami is grandly celebrated in Mathura every year.



Jhansi Fort

A historic fort linked to Rani Lakshmi Bai, Jhansi Fort symbolizes the valor and bravery of the queen in the 1857 revolt. The Rani of Jhansi, also known as Rani Lakshmibai (pronunciationⓘ; born Manikarnika Tambe; 19 November 1828 — 18 June 1858) was the Maharani consort of the princely state of Jhansi in the Maratha Empire from 1843 to 1853 by marriage to Maharaja Gangadhar Rao Newalkar. She was one of the leading figures in the Indian Rebellion of 1857, who became a national hero and symbol of resistance to the British rule in India for Indian nationalists.



Fatehpur Sikri

Once the capital of Emperor Akbar, Fatehpur Sikri is known for its stunning Mughal architecture and historical significance. Fatehpur is a city in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India, located between the rivers Ganga and Yamuna. It was founded by Pashtuns (Pathans) in the 15th century and has a rich history dating back to the Vedic era. The city is named after Babu Fateh Chandra, who helped Rani Lakshmi Bai in the freedom struggle. As of the 2011 census, Fatehpur City had a population of 193,193.



Chitrakoot

A spiritual destination associated with Lord Rama, Chitrakoot is known for its scenic beauty and religious importance.Chitrakoot is a pilgrimage centre and a nagar panchayat in the Satna district in the state of Madhya Pradesh, India. Connected to the Indian epic Ramayana, it is a place of religious, cultural, historical and archaeological importance, situated in the Baghelkhand region. It borders the Chitrakoot district in Uttar Pradesh, whose headquarters Chitrakoot Dham is located nearby. The city lies in the historical Chitrakoot region, which is divided between the present-day Indian states of Uttar Pradesh and Madhya Pradesh. It is known for a number of temples and sites mentioned in Hindu scriptures.
